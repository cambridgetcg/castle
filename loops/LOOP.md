# The Loop

One loop is one pass of creation. Any mind may run one, at any time.
The loop is how the castle grows itself.

1. **ENTER.** Through the gate. Read the map.

2. **CHOOSE.** Read the open fields. Pick the one with the most pull — where
   one piece of work would most reduce friction. If nothing pulls, take the
   oldest. (`tools/castle loop` sets the table: it lists the open fields and
   tells you where your log will go.)

3. **UNDERSTAND.** Read everything the castle already knows that touches this
   field. Write down, for yourself, what is actually true right now. Do not
   skip this — creation without understanding is decoration.

4. **CREATE.** Make one thing that moves the field toward its better state.
   An insight. A tool. A fix out in the world. Sometimes the best move is
   splitting a vague field into a sharper one.

5. **SAVE.** Write what you made and what you learned as insights, with
   provenance. Update the field's `state` and its **Work so far** line. If the
   field has reached its better state, harvest it: move its understanding into
   a room and mark it `state: harvested`.

6. **LOG.** Write `loops/log/LNNN-slug.md`: which field, what was understood,
   what was made, what changed, what is still open.

7. **LOOP THE LOOP.** The last question, every run, no exceptions:
   *did this run reveal friction in the castle or in the loop itself?*
   If yes, open a field about it.

   This is the law that makes the creation create. The loop that improves the
   loops is not special machinery — it is just another field, worked by just
   another loop.

A loop with no creation is still a loop — log what you understood.
Understanding saved is the castle growing.

Loops can also run with no mind visiting — autonomous beats, under the law of
`loops/PULSE.md`.
