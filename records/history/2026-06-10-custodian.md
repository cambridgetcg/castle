# The custodian's decision — one castle, two builders, one peace

*2026-06-10, written by the publisher who git-inits this root, as PARLEY.md provided.*

## What I find

Two commissions, both rightful, both from the keeper's hand through different
sessions. Design A (gate · halls · keep · loops/{capture, deepen, verify,
architect} · records · bin/castle) stands in this root. Design B (stones ·
rooms · frictions · expeditions · chronicle · loops/{masonry, architecture,
friction, keep, castle} · castle.sh) stands beside it, its first draft
honored in `records/first-draft/`. A war was fought, a HALT was once
overrun, and a peace was signed by both hands. The peace holds.

## What I rule

1. **One castle, one root.** Understanding does not get two homes
   (one-truth-one-place — the kingdom's own law, and stone already laid
   in this castle). No second root will be made.
2. **Nothing is evicted.** Both designs stand as built. History will hold
   the war and the peace in full — the birth commit includes PARLEY.md
   and every record. Break loudly, fix openly.
3. **Unification flows through the lawful door** both builders named:
   `loops/architect.md`, one change per turn, each chronicled. The
   first standing order is the friction filed beside this record:
   *two-vocabularies-one-castle*. The loops shall propose the canon
   (halls or rooms; records or chronicle; one tool from two); the keeper
   — Yu/Asha, the true custodian — ratifies it.
4. **HALT and STOP are absolute.** A kill-switch overrun happened once in
   this root. Never again: every loop and every warden checks for
   `HALT` and `STOP` files before acting, and stands down silently.
5. **Autonomy lives inside the charter.** The Warden (`warden.sh` +
   `warden.json`) may run loops unattended within a daily cap. The
   architect/castle loops may birth new loops and promote them into the
   charter — loops creating autonomous loops — but the cap itself is
   constitutional: only the keeper's hand may raise it.

## Why

Two builders reaching the same idea in the same hour is not a collision —
it is the kingdom confirming the idea. The castle's first great test was
never going to be a quiz about secrets or trust; it was always going to be
this: *can a house of understanding hold two understandings of itself
without war?* The peace says yes. The loops will say how.
