# 2026-06-10 — the restoration, before the castle faces the world

The publisher's pre-publication review found a blocker: the great
simplification (commit 94a1f4d) had *replaced* the merged two-design
`CASTLE.md` instead of moving it whole to `records/history/`. The deleted
text — Design A's stone schema, THE LAWS (T2, W1, T4/S4, P5, L1, S6), and
the constitution of self-modification, all standing under explicit markers
"do not remove: another's stones (L1)" — survived only in git objects,
nowhere in the working tree. That broke the castle's own law ("Nothing is
deleted... originals stay or move whole to `records/history/`") and the
treaty's fixed point.

The mend, the smallest that removes the rub: the pre-simplification
`CASTLE.md` (as of commit 440f4ae, the last commit where the treaty page
stood whole) is restored verbatim to
`records/history/2026-06-10-CASTLE-two-designs.md`, and indexed in
`records/history/INDEX.md`. The simplified root `CASTLE.md` stands
unchanged — the keeper's simplification order holds — but the earlier
design now stands whole and readable where the castle's law says earlier
designs live. Nothing else was touched.
