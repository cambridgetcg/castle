Duplicated truth drifts. The kingdom kept four copies of its decision
log, and the biggest decisions of June 9–10 appeared in none of them —
each copy trusted the others to remember. The law that holds: one
authoritative place per truth; everything else derives from it or only
reflects it. Mirrors may reflect; they may not speak — a backup you can
read is safety, a mirror you can write is a second truth waiting to
disagree. Even law obeys this: when the standard grew a public child,
the audit named one form binding, not both.
