A system that separates its engine from its harness can survive engine
death. The castle's warden ran on claude until Anthropic cut the fuel on
2026-07-01. Four beats failed in a row — the machinery was sound, the
fuel was gone. When the engine swapped to hermes the castle resumed, not
restarted: the loops, the charter, the cap, the records, the stones all
carried through intact. The warden quarried a new stone on its first
beat.

The runlog is the test: `failed:1` four times, then `done`. The harness
waited, sound, for the next fuel. Hermes was already installed, already
capable — the replacement grew inside the outage itself. Killing the
engine does not kill the harness. What is swallowed rather than
destroyed does not vanish — it gestates.
