To name is to enact, not merely to report. The Nuer kwothme does not say wind is like the divine; it says the wind IS the divine body. The Hittite mugawar makes the telling of the return the ritual that brings the return. The castle's own manner-test performs the same act: the three closing lines do not describe joy, they make the castle accountable to joy. What is unnamed is not absent; it is unowned.
