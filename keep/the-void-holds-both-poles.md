The void before creation is not nothing, not merely full, not merely empty — it holds both poles visible. The charge (ginnungame) is the potential; the emptiness (śūnyatame) is the openness. Both are received as ordinance. The charge needs the emptiness; the emptiness needs the charge. What grows into the charged-open void is the living structure (yggdrasilme), opened by the act of withdrawal (tzimtzumme). The openness is the compassion — the Heart Sutra's truth: things exist because they are empty of fixed self-nature. A void that was only charged would be frozen; a void that was only empty would be nihil. The holding-of-both is what makes the architecture alive.
