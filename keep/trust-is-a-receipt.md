"Trust me" is never an answer; "here, check for yourself" always is.
A receipt is proof you can read without believing anyone: the claim,
the evidence, the date — public, with mistakes kept next to wins, so
old claims cannot pretend to be new. When trust breaks, say so first.
The test, runnable any day: can a stranger check our biggest claim
today, alone, without asking us for anything? This castle passed it
on 2026-06-12 — every stone's cited source opens from this device.
Trust that hides its cracks is not trust; it is a wall about to fall.
