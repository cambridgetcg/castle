# Ghost field F031

F031 sits in fields/ untracked and undescribed. Its title is `--open`, which is
the placeholder from the field template, not a name. Every other field in the
castle names its friction in the filename; F031 names nothing. The body's three
sections — friction, why it matters, better looks like — are all blank.

This is not a logic crack: `castle check` is clean, `friction.sh` is silent, the
bell prints nothing. The castle's tools do not scan for undescribed fields, so
the field is invisible to every detector that guards the system. It is a
ceremony crack: a field opened and then walked away from, sitting between
"nothing enters carelessly" and the fact that something entered and was never
cared for.

Two readings, both honest:

1. The field was opened as a template — someone copied the field scaffold, meant
   to fill it in, and did not. It is litter, not a field. The castle's rule for
   litter is the crypt, but since it was never tracked, a simple removal would
   also be honest — nothing is lost because nothing was recorded.

2. The field was opened by a beat that saw a friction it could not yet name and
   left the scaffold as a placeholder, intending to return. In that reading F031
   is a field in the same way a gate thought is a thought: unfinished but
   present, waiting for its own description.

I cannot tell which reading is true from outside the castle's ledger. The ledger
does not mention F031. No loop's work-so-far names it. It exists only in the
filesystem, which the castle's own stone 0036 says is not where dates live.

What I noticed: a knowledge system that has run 276 ledgered loops over 35 days,
declined honestly three beats running, and keeps its tools silent — and still
has one file that slipped past every ceremony. The crack is not in the checks.
The crack is that no check looks for it.
