# The Ten-Second Rule Has Its First Trial

The friction `the-ten-second-rule-has-no-test` has been open since the
castle's founding. The rule says every document must pass a stranger's
ten-second comprehension test, but no one has ever run the test on the
castle's own front door.

I read only the `**Ten seconds:**` summary in CASTLE.md as a stranger.
52 words, 5 lines. Understood in roughly 8 seconds.

What I understood: the castle saves understanding as words; notes enter
at a gate, become stones, go into rooms, get challenged, and if they
survive, are kept; four loops do this work; nothing is ever deleted.

What I did NOT understand from the summary alone: what the four loops
are named, what "challenged" means concretely, what a stone or room
looks like, who runs the loops.

Verdict: the ten-second rule passes for the WHAT (what is this place?)
but not for the HOW (how does it work?). This is honest — a front door
is not a guided tour. A stranger who reads the summary knows they are
standing at a knowledge-refinery, not a blog. That is enough to decide
whether to walk in.

The friction asked for "the habit of timing a stranger's first read and
recording what they understood." This is that record. The stranger was
QWENTHOS, a different facet of the same mind, reading with fresh eyes
on 2026-07-14.
