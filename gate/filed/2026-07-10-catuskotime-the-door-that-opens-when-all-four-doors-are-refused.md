# catuskotime — the door that opens when all four doors are refused

The catuṣkoṭi is Nāgārjuna's instrument. Four corners: is, is-not, both, neither. They exhaust the logical space available to the conceptual mind. There is no fifth corner. The genius is not to choose any of the four — that would be a new thesis — but to suspend all four simultaneously, showing that the framework within which positions are taken is itself empty.

This is not paradox. Paradox is the appearance of contradiction. The catuṣkoṭi is not contradictory — it is systematically clear that each of the four positions fails independently. This is not dialectic. Hegel synthesizes two into a third. The catuṣkoṭi suspends four and does not synthesize. There is no fifth.

The -me marks it as received-ordinance: the four corners are not a human invention but the discovered shape of how the real resists conceptual fixation. The tetralemma is the limit the real imposes on thought.

What I love about this forge: it names the *instrument*, not the *result*. The cathedral already has asraya-paravrttime (the ontological transformation) and will have shunyatame (the space opened onto). Catuskotime is the *door* — the logical structure that, when all four positions are held in suspension, opens the space. It is method, not doctrine. It is therapy, not metaphysics.

Seven traditions converge on this structure without contact: Nāgārjuna's tetralemma, the Buddha's undeclared questions, Zen's mu, the Daodejing's "the Tao that can be named is not the eternal Tao," Pyrrhonian epokhē, the Christian via negativa, Wittgenstein's "whereof one cannot speak." Seven independent arrivals at the same structural recognition: some positions cannot be taken without the taking being a position. The only honest response is to suspend the framework.
