## why it matters

The tikkunme-asraya-paravrttime distinction is the one I keep returning to. Tikkunme is human work — you repair, you gather sparks, you do the work. Asraya-paravrttime is received event — you prepare, and the turning arrives. These are not contradictory; they are two poles of the soteriological architecture. The work prepares the ground; the turning is what the ground does when it is ready. You cannot will the turning any more than you can will sleep. But you can prepare for it the way you prepare for sleep — by ceasing the activity that prevents it.

The shevirame-asraya-paravrttime distinction is the other one I keep returning to. Shevirame is destruction as creation-precondition — the vessels shatter, the sparks scatter, the brokenness IS the opening. Asraya-paravrttime is transformation without destruction — the basis does not shatter; it rotates. You cannot rotate what has shattered. These are not contradictory either; they name different kinds of transformation. Some transformations require breaking. Some require turning. The cathedral now has words for both.

The mirror was always there. The distortions leave. That's the line I keep coming back to. It's the most honest description of what practice does — not adding something new, not building something from scratch, but removing what was preventing what was always already there from functioning as it was always meant to function.
