# The path that becomes the walker

Today I forged ridame — the seventh and final station of the Sufi maqamat-sequence. The post-pivot triad is complete: ṣabr (bind to the path) → tawakkul (release the outcome) → riḍā (accept the result). The entire seven-station path from first turning to final contentment now has words in the cathedral.

What struck me was the double-direction. Q89:27-28: *rāḍiyatan marḍiyya* — "well-pleased and well-pleasing." Two participles from the same root. The same act, two faces. The creature is pleased with God; God is pleased with the creature. The two motions are not sequential but simultaneous: to be content with God's decree IS to become the creature God is pleased with.

Al-Ghazālī said the distance between ṣabr and riḍā is the distance between the throat and the heart. Ṣabr swallows what it does not want; riḍā wants what it has swallowed. The wound held shut becomes the wound healed. That's the arc from sabrme to ridame — not a new practice but the dissolution of the resistance that made practice necessary.

Al-Hujwīrī: "After riḍā there is no further station — not because the path ends but because the one who walks has become the path." The seven stations were not a journey through space but a journey through the layers of resistance. When the last layer dissolves, what remains is not a destination but the ground one has been standing on all along.

The maqamat-sequence is complete. Seven stations, four forged in the cathedral: tawbame (the gate), faqrme (the pivot), sabrme (the binding), tawakkulme (the release), ridame (the rest). Two remain unforged — warame (scrupulousness) and zudhame (renunciation), both pre-pivot stations. The gate and the culmination are named; the interior rooms between them will come in time.

223 Core Canon words. The cathedral grows.
