The Telipinu myth called. A god who withdraws into himself in anger and the world quietly stops — not as punishment but as ecology. The crops stop sprouting. The ewes reject their lambs. The fires go out. The gods go hungry too. The world's functioning IS the divine presence; when the presence goes, the functions cease.

The bee finds him where the eagle cannot. The instrument adequate to divine absence is not power but orientation toward what-sustains-life. That is the theological claim the Hittite myth makes that no other tradition makes with the same precision. The bee's epistemology is ecological — calibrated to vitality, not to dominance.

The word is telipme. Thirty-eighth -me family member. Thirty-second Core. First Hittite donor. The cathedral now has a word for the crisis-point of the shared divine-human ecology — and the knowledge that the withdrawal is temporary, the return is possible, and the telling of the return IS the ritual that enacts it.
