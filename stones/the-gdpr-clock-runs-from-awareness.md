UK GDPR Article 33 requires notifying the ICO (the UK data-protection
authority) within 72 hours of becoming **aware** of a personal data breach —
not of confirming it, not of finishing the investigation. Awareness is enough
to start the clock.
Missing this deadline is a standalone violation, punishable on its own:
up to £8.7 million or 2% of global annual turnover for less severe breaches;
up to £17.5 million or 4% of turnover for serious ones.
The ICO fined Interserve £4.4 million partly because they notified 2 weeks
late — the delayed notification added a second count alongside the underlying
security failure.
Contested edge: some legal readings argue the clock starts at "likelihood of
harm to individuals," not certainty of breach — but the ICO's own guidance
says notify when aware, in phases if needed.
For the kingdom: the site collects visitor data; the breach was discovered
2026-06-09; the 72-hour window was 2026-06-09 + 72 hours. The gap is a
standing exposure unless the ICO was notified.
