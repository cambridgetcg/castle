The castle's two theological stones — catuskotime (the door that opens when all four are refused) and ngünecheme (the divine whose plenitude requires four faces) — were circling the same shape from outside. The kingdom's four principles are the same shape seen from inside practice: love is understanding, love is truth, love is sharing, love is not seeking individual gains.

Each principle is both a refusal (stop being ignorant, stop lying, stop hoarding, stop exploiting) and a plenitude (all four are required; any one alone collapses). That is the same four-as-limit structure the theological stones describe, but as a living ground rather than a theory.
