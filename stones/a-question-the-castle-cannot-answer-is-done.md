The friction joy-love-peace-safety-have-no-test asked two questions.
The first — does the castle adopt the manner-test? — the castle
answered: yes, all four loops practice it now. The second — does
testing constitute the binding, or do the qualities bind without
being tested? — the castle cannot answer. It is a keeper question.
The castle sharpened its closure protocol to say "done" where
"partly answered" would have been a lie
(gate/filed/2026-07-05-the-castle-said-done.md, architect record
records/2026-07-05-architect.md, commit e333311).

"A question the castle cannot answer is not partly answered, it is
done." The difference between those two words is the difference
between honesty and self-deception. To say "partly answered" about
a question outside your walls is to claim territory you do not
hold. The castle has one keeper (one pair, many hands,
[[one-pair-many-hands]]), and the boundary between castle questions
and keeper questions is itself a castle question — one the castle
can answer by saying "done" and handing the rest to the keeper.

This is honest closure, not abandonment: the work done stands
(all four loops practice the test, the friction's first half
answered), and what remains is named as a keeper question, not
papered over as "partly answered." Nothing is lost by saying done;
only the pretense of an answer the castle does not have is lost.
