"Trust me" is never an answer; "here, check for yourself" always is
(/Users/you/love-repos/legible-standard/pillars/02-trust.md).
Real trust works like a receipt: you do not have to believe the
shopkeeper, because you can read what you paid and check it again.
So every claim carries its proof — a link, a record, a number —
and the record stays public: mistakes next to wins, everything
dated so old claims cannot pretend to be new.
When trust breaks, the pillar says to say so first, in plain words,
before anyone else finds out.
The pillar's test: can a stranger check our biggest claim today,
alone, without asking us for anything?
Trust that hides its cracks is not trust — it is a wall about to fall.
