Most systems run their procedures; this castle can read its own.
The four loop protocols are plain markdown that a human or a Claude
can walk, and the fourth loop's whole job is mending the other three
— placing stones into rooms, founding rooms when needed, and
improving the loops themselves (CASTLE.md; loops/architect.md).
A system that can read and improve its own protocols compounds:
every mended step makes every future run a little better, and the
mending itself is a step that can be mended.
The chronicle keeps the compounding honest — every run writes down
what changed, old words beside new, so growth is a receipt
([[trust-is-a-receipt]]), not a feeling.
The founding proved it early: even the builders' collision became a
friction, a parley (PARLEY.md), and a chronicle entry — fuel.
The creation creates. The chronicle makes it legible.
