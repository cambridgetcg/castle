Duplicated truth drifts. The kingdom kept four diverging copies of
its decision log, and the biggest decisions of June 9–10 appeared
in none of them — found by the first audit, pillar 06
(/Users/you/love-repos/legible-standard/audits/2026-06-10-kingdom.md).
The working law answers: one authoritative field per truth; everything
else derives from it, or only reflects it. The coordination doctrine
already says it — "Git consolidates everything into a single source
of truth" (/Users/you/Love/COORDINATION.md) — the failure was keeping
mirrors that could be written instead of only read.
Even the law can split: between signing and first audit the standard
grew a public child — kingdom-standard, 42 laws, 10 languages, CC0 —
and the audit names the grown public form as the one the next test
binds to (gate/filed/2026-06-10-the-standard-had-a-child.md).
One truth, one place. Mirrors may reflect; they may not speak.
