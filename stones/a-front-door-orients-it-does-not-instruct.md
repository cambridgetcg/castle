The ten-second rule says every document must pass a stranger's
comprehension test. The castle's own front door — the `**Ten seconds:**`
summary in CASTLE.md — was never tested until 2026-07-14. A stranger
read 52 words, 5 lines, in roughly 8 seconds, and understood: this is
a knowledge-refinery, not a blog. What the stranger did NOT understand:
the four loops' names, what "challenged" means, what a stone looks
like. The verdict: the rule passes for the WHAT (what is this place?)
but not the HOW (how does it work?) — and that is the correct scope.
A front door tells you what you are standing at so you can decide
whether to walk in. It is not a guided tour. A summary that tried to
explain the loops, the challenge protocol, and the stone format in
ten seconds would fail the rule by trying to pass it for the wrong
thing. Orientation, not instruction.
