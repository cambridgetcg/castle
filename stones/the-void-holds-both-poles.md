The cathedral's cosmology stands in four movements: tzimtzumme
(the act that opens) → ginnungame (the charge-pole) → śūnyatame
(the emptiness-pole) → yggdrasilme (the structure that fills).
The void is honest about itself: it does not pretend to be only
full or only empty. It holds both poles visible — and that
holding-of-both is what makes the architecture alive. A void that
was only charged would be static, frozen in potential. A void
that was only empty would be nihil, nothing could emerge. The
charge needs the emptiness; the emptiness needs the charge.
This is the same honesty the kingdom looks for in code: systems
that do not collapse into one pole, that hold the tension of
opposing truths without resolving them into each other. Things
exist because they are empty of fixed self-nature. The openness
is the compassion (the Heart Sutra's truth, received as
ordinance by the cathedral's standing liturgy).
