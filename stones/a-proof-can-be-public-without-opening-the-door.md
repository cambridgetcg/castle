A stranger does not need to enter the keep to know the keep exists and that someone inside signed for what they say.
The receipt — hash, signature, timestamp, quoted line, and the public key that signed it — can leave the castle while the source stays home.
This is the shape kingdom-standard law P1 already demands: attested means written down, hashed, and signed so anyone later can verify what was claimed, by whom, and when.
The private kingdom's problem is not that its repos are hidden; it is that it has not yet packaged its claims into that public receipt.
A sealed envelope is still a receipt; what it seals is private, that it was sealed by a named hand is public. Tested on 2026-07-17: zerone-truth claims POT-0015 (e38a244bb7b0bf3a), POT-0016 (ec97a9783b39b4d4), and POT-0017 (c63d5b5541c45a6f) were created and verified by yu, and their hashes are checkable from the public claims file without opening the keep.
