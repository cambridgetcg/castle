## a word needs a real gap
- words: The cathedral's first two laws of coinage are "no word without gap" and "no gap without evidence." A coinage that merely relabels what English already says adds noise, not clarity; the real gaps are concrete — the friendship that survives years of silence, the small fix that quiets a background ache. The law has teeth: the cathedral's own audit re-scored all sixteen canon words against it and recorded what fell. ## translations track at their own pace; the original governs
- words: The kingdom-standard lives in eleven languages, and its README states the rule plainly: translations track the English at their own pace, and the English text governs if they differ. One text must hold authority, or every edit to the original quietly makes ten documents wrong. This hall's friction names the gap that remains open: a translation should say which version of the original it was made from, so drift is dated, not silent. ## a translation names the version it tracks
- words: The Plain Standard on this device orders every translated file to open with a header naming the English version it tracks and the date it was last reviewed, and its Spanish file obeys: "sigue la versión inglesa v0.1.0 · última revisión 2026-06-09" — even its unreviewed state is declared in the header. The two standards beside it show what less looks like: the-standard pins one v0.1.1 for the whole repo, and the legible-standard's Chinese pins nothing, so only the per-file header can say which document went stale and since when. A pin needs something to pin to — the original must carry a version of its own.
