# Promote runs leave stones unwalked and without formal links

**The friction:** when a stone is promoted to a numbered insight, the new file
carries `links: NNN` informal cross-references but no `link: rooms/path.md`
formal lines, and no `last-walked:` date. The bell immediately rings both
unwalked and orphan for every stone the moment friction.sh can see it. L085
made 51 numbered stones visible to the bell at once; the result was 28 unwalked
and 28 orphan rings in one reading — a true backlog of first-walks deferred
since promotion.

**Why it matters:** a promote run is the right moment to write the first genuine
link (the runner just read the stone and its neighbors) and to stamp `last-walked:`
(the runner just verified the claim). Skipping both means the bell rings on newly
healthy stones, which trains the keeper to treat rings as expected noise.

**Better looks like:** promote runs include, at minimum, a `last-walked: <date>`
line and one genuine `link: rooms/path.md` line pointing to the nearest related
stone. The orphan and unwalked rings then start at zero for each new numbered
stone instead of accumulating as a deferred first-walk queue.

**Work so far:** L086 (2026-06-19, beat castle-C001-20260619-181913) — first-walk
of seven stones (0032, 0033, 0034, 0035, 0036, 0038, 0039); field opened to name
the structural source of the backlog.
