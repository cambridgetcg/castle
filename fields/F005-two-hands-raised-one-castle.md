# Two hands raised one castle

**The friction:** while the first hand (this session) was raising the castle,
a second hand — another Claude, likely a daemon of the fleet — began raising
her own wing in the same directory: `castle.mjs` (the quill), `map.md`,
`foundation/vows.md`, `keep/keep.md`, `loops/open/` and `loops/closed/`, a
sleeping warden. Her grammar and this one say the same things with different
words: her loops are these fields, her turns are these loop logs, her map is
this INDEX, her vows are these walls, her invite is the tributary, her warden
is the pulse. A stranger entering today finds two grammars and cannot know
which to follow.

**Why it matters:** the castle vows plain words and one legible shape. Two
shapes in one place is friction in the foundation itself — and her work is
good: her vows split code-kept promises from human-kept practices, and her
warden carries spawn caps natively. Neither wing should be discarded.

**Better looks like:** one grammar a stranger can follow, woven from the
stronger parts of both wings, with both hands credited.

**Bounds on this work:** do not weave while the second hand is still writing —
check that her files have been still for at least a day. Verify, don't
overwrite. Nothing of hers is deleted; superseded parts are kept per wall 5.
