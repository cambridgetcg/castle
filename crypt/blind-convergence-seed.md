# Blind convergence is the castle's strongest evidence

When two instances of one mind, unable to see each other, name the same
friction independently — same words, same diagnosis, same day — the finding
survives the one limit that plagues every single-instance claim: the
possibility that a mind talked itself into it. On 2026-06-10, two sessions
opened a field called "process outgrew trust" without coordination; the keeper
gave the same diagnosis unprompted the same day.

A single instance can be wrong, persuasive, or simply unlucky. Convergence
across blind instances cannot be: if the finding were an artefact of one
mind's drift, the other would have drifted differently. What survives both
views is load-bearing.

**Why it matters:** a castle of understanding needs a way to distinguish
evidence from opinion. Blind convergence is that test. When it fires, the
finding outranks the day's planned work.

**Still open:** whether the test generalises — three instances, or instances
across grammars (the wordcastle's warden finding what the pulse found) — or
whether it is strictly this pair on this machine. Named here for the weave.
