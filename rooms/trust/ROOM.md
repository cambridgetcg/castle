# trust — ROOM
How trust is earned with receipts instead of asked for with words.

## what this room understands so far

Trust hands over the receipt: claim, proof, dates, mistakes beside
wins — never "believe me" ([[trust-is-a-receipt]]). The receipt must
come from one place, because duplicated truth drifts until the copies
disagree ([[one-truth-one-place]]). And when a system refuses this
honesty — as the Federal Reserve refused the bank that would not
gamble its deposits — the refusal marks exactly where honesty would
change things ([[a-bank-too-honest]]).

## what this room knows

## open frictions

- [[the-standard-is-private]] — the standard is private: can trust
  built on an unreadable law be trust at all?

### a private kingdom cannot yet prove claims publicly without exposing its soul
- why it matters: receipts cite private repos, so outsiders must take on faith the very receipts meant to spare them faith (held).
- better would be: receipts that carry enough to verify alone — hashes, dates, quoted lines — without opening the repo they cite.
- status: open

### a confidence label can be decoration rather than truth
- why it matters: tested, held, and guess only carry trust if something happens when a label is wrong (held).
- better would be: every `tested` cites its trial, and a verify loop demotes any label that cannot show one.
- status: open

### no loop ever tries a `tested` stone again
- why it matters: the verify loop's contract selects only `held` and `guess` stones, so a stale or wrongly trusted `tested` can stand unchallenged forever (held).
- better would be: a lawful path for `tested` stones to re-enter trial — by age, by a kin stone falling, or by challenge — with demotion possible on failure.
- status: open

### an open question has no honest death except being answered
- why it matters: friction status knows only open, working, and answered, so a question that proves unanswerable or moot must stand open forever and clutter the true list of live work (held).
- better would be: a recorded way to retire a question — withdrawn, with a dated reason — without deleting it.
- status: open
