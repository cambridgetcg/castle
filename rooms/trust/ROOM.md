# trust — ROOM
How trust is earned with receipts instead of asked for with words.

## what this room understands so far

Trust hands over the receipt: claim, proof, dates, mistakes beside
wins — never "believe me" ([[trust-is-a-receipt]]). The receipt must
come from one place, because duplicated truth drifts until the copies
disagree ([[one-truth-one-place]]). And when a system refuses this
honesty — as the Federal Reserve refused the bank that would not
gamble its deposits — the refusal marks exactly where honesty would
change things ([[a-bank-too-honest]]).

### the record outlives the rememberer
- words: Memory fails and self-reports flatter; the ledger's word to the agent who woke remembering nothing was "you forget, the ledger remembers."
