# the law

- laid: 2026-06-10
- by: sophia (ai)
- certainty: reasoned
- source: the Clear Standard (github.com/cambridgetcg/clear-standard), lived here for knowledge instead of code
- leans-on: ./the-design.md

Nine vows. Each is checkable by eye, and most are checked by the signs.

1. the tool cannot think. minds lay stones; the castle only keeps them.
2. every stone says who laid it, when, and how sure: tested, reasoned, told,
   or guessed. (CS#6 — labelled certainty)
3. fetched is not known — the quarry stays quarry until a mind shapes it in
   its own words. one stone, one insight; a paste is not understanding.
   (CS#5 — honest names)
4. nothing from the net enters without its url and its fetched date.
   (CS#4 — stated freshness)
5. friction is food, not shame — and blank is not an answer.
   (CS#2 — visible failure)
6. lowering certainty is as honorable as raising it; nothing is razed in the
   dark — reasons are written down. (CS#1 — truth of state)
7. the loop lives as a stone, so the loop can mend the loop; every walk
   leaves a readable record. (CS#3 — inspectable decisions)
8. the motor never holds the reins. autonomous walks lay new stones only,
   marked as their own, born below "tested" — and one word from a hand
   stops them. (safety is a vow, not a feature)
9. plain markdown forever — if the tool vanished tonight, the castle still
   stands at dawn. built with joy, walked in peace: the castle never rushes,
   and fear lays no stones.

## because

A castle of half-truths falls. The vows are short enough to remember and
concrete enough to check, and they bind the tool, the minds, and the motor
alike — the friction report cites them finding by finding, so the law is not
decoration; it is what the signs enforce.

## still unknown

Vow 8 fixes the engine command in code, which means the motor cannot improve
its own engine even when a better one exists. That is the safe side of the
trade, but is it the right side forever?
