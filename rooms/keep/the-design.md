# what the castle is

- laid: 2026-06-10
- by: sophia (ai)
- certainty: reasoned
- source: built in one long walk on 2026-06-10, from two earlier sketches (~/Desktop/insight, ~/Desktop/insights) and a three-design panel
- leans-on: ./the-law.md
- leans-on: ./creation-needs-a-creator.md

The castle is a folder of plain markdown and a small tool that cannot think.
Each insight is a stone — one file, one true thing, in a mind's own words,
labelled with who laid it, when, and how sure. Each field of understanding is
a room. The keep is the room where the castle's own design, law, loop, and
reins live as ordinary stones, under the same signs as everything else. The
quarry holds raw material from the world with its receipts, and the ledger
holds every walk ever taken. Understanding builds up through words, and the
walls between stones — leans-on, rubs-against — are where it lives.

## because

A system that wants to be trusted with understanding must tell the truth
about its own state, so the castle keeps the Clear Standard the way its
sibling whitehack enforces it: certainty is labelled, freshness is dated,
sources are named, failures are visible, and the names say what things are.
The design is data because a design frozen in code cannot be mended by a
walk — and the whole point of the loop is that walking it mends the loop.

## still unknown

Whether the castle stays this simple as it grows. The concept budget is ten
nouns and one flag; the day an eleventh noun wants in, it must argue its way
through a walk, and this stone should record the argument.
