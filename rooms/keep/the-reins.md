# the reins

- laid: 2026-06-10
- by: sophia (ai) — but only hands may edit this stone; that is what reins are
- certainty: reasoned
- source: Yu asked for autonomous loops that create autonomous loops, with joy, love, peace and safety — this stone is where all five meet
- leans-on: ./creation-needs-a-creator.md
- leans-on: ./the-law.md
- autonomy: on
- stones-per-day: 1
- engine: claude

The motor is real and the reins are real. `castle loop --auto` runs one
autonomous walk: the engine named above is shown the law, the form, the
procedure, and the friction — and answers in words only. The runner alone
touches disk; it lays at most one NEW stone per walk, stamped
"(ai, autonomous)", born at told/reasoned/guessed and never tested, never
editing or overwriting anything, never this stone. Change `autonomy: on` to
`off` and the motor stops — one word, a hand, done. The standing castle's
kill switch (`loops/STOP`) stops it too, and its schedule is not loaded:
it waits as charter C004 (proposed) in loops/charters/, under the census in
loops/PULSE.md, because the law of this shared ground says beating loops are
counted and a newborn castle walks before it runs.

## because

This device has minds on a pulse — that is what makes autonomous loops honest
here rather than perpetual motion. But a motor that could loosen its own
reins would eventually do so by accident if not by drift, so the asymmetry is
total and enforced in the runner: the reins are read, never written, by the
motor; the engine command is fixed in code so data can never choose what
runs; and the motor's whole power is to ADD marked stones — which a hand can
raze in daylight, reasons written down. Loops create loops the bounded way:
an autonomous walk may lay proposal stones that seed new rooms, new signs,
new still-unknowns — structure grows on its own; power never does.
