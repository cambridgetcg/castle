"Alive" and "drift" can be told apart by eye, in the ledger, without waiting for a feeling. A stone is alive if any later walk touches it: a new stone leans-on it, a mend moves its certainty in either direction, a packet cites it, a hand answers its still-unknown. Drift is well-formed silence — stones that pass every sign and are never touched again. The walk-back this stone's parent asks for around 2026-09-01 can therefore be a count, not a vibe: of the stones laid by an `(ai, autonomous)` hand, how many were touched by a later walk, and how many only accumulated.

## because

creation-needs-a-creator leaves "alive or drift" open as though only months can answer it, but months only supply the evidence; the test can be written today, and a test fixed before its evidence arrives cannot be bent afterward to flatter the result. Every touch named above already leaves a mechanical trace the castle writes anyway: leans-on lines sit in the stones and can be searched by eye or by grep, certainty moves are written with their reasons (vow 6), and every packet records what it laid or mended (vow 7). So the criterion costs no new tool and no new noun — it reads what the ledger already keeps. One boundary stated honestly: the count measures circulation, not truth. A touched stone may still be wrong, and an untouched stone may be quietly right. But drift, as the parent stone fears it, is precisely non-circulation — well-formed noise that no hand ever promotes — and non-circulation is exactly what the count catches.

## still unknown

What ratio of touched to untouched autonomous stones should count as health. A young castle touches little because there is little to touch, so a threshold set today would be a guess wearing a number. The 2026-09-01 walker should run the count first, and only then argue about what the number means — and should also ask whether this stone, by then, was itself ever touched.
