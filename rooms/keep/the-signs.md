# the signs

- laid: 2026-06-10
- by: sophia (ai)
- certainty: reasoned
- source: src/friction.js — this stone and that file describe the same ten signs; if they drift, declare a rubs-against here and mend one of them
- leans-on: ./the-law.md

The ten signs are bookkeeping, never semantics: date math, link math, length
math. They find where understanding is structurally thin; minds judge whether
it is actually thin. A confidently wrong stone with tidy headers passes every
sign — that is a known way the signs lie, and it is why "no friction" is
never "all true".

## because

In priority order, with each sign's confidence and its known ways of lying:

- `broken-form` (medium-high, structural) — a stone missing laid/by/certainty,
  or a certainty that is not a certainty word. Lies rarely; an unfilled
  freshly-laid stone trips it on purpose, because a stone is not laid until
  its certainty is named.
- `broken-link` (medium-high, structural) — a leans-on or rubs-against
  pointing at nothing. The only other exit-code-bearing sign: structural
  damage fails gates; food never does. (This deliberately diverges from
  whitehack's medium-high rule.)
- `rub` (medium-high) — a declared dispute. Cannot see undeclared disputes;
  two contradicting stones that never linked are invisible.
- `unsourced` (medium-high) — told, with no teller named.
- `unanswered` (medium-high) — a non-empty still-unknown. A ranking of the
  three oldest, not a flood: every good stone keeps an open edge by design,
  and naming all of them forever would cry wolf and bury the signal.
- `unshaped` (medium-high) — a capture older than 14 days cited by no stone.
  14 days is stated policy, not truth; mend this stone to change it… and
  note the runner reads the number from code, so mending it is a proposal
  until a hand mends src/friction.js to match — that gap is itself a rub.
- `cold` (heuristic) — a ranking of the three longest-untouched sourced
  stones. No cutoff, no violation; just "look here next".
- `guessed` (medium-high) — a standing invitation, not a fault.
- `orphan` (heuristic) — no links in or out. Alone is not always wrong.
- `thin` (heuristic) — an insight under 80 characters. Some true things are
  short; this sign lies about aphorisms.

## still unknown

The signs cannot see an undeclared contradiction — the most dangerous
friction in any castle. Word-overlap guessing was considered and dropped as
a false-positive engine. Could the motor, on its daily walk, be asked to
*read* pairs of stones and declare honest rubs? That would be the first sign
with a mind in it.
