# Loops may create loops, but only inside walls

The castle's loops can run autonomously, and an autonomous beat may draft and
even instantiate new autonomous loops — that is law 7 of `loops/PULSE.md`.
The walls that make this safe: a STOP file that silences everything at zero
cost, a census capped at three beating loops, a charter that must name
cadence, budget, stop, and bounds before it may beat, and reversible work
only. Recursion is the growth ([[0003]]); the walls are why the growth is
safe to want.

**Why it matters:** unbounded self-replication is the classic failure of
self-improving systems. The castle gets the creation that creates, and Yu
keeps one file and one command that end it all.
