# A knowledge system's first stones describe itself

**Claim.** A knowledge system builds understanding of its own structure before
building understanding of the world; the early self-referential ratio is
calibration, not indulgence.

**How it ripened.** First observed at L034 (2026-06-19): 26 insights in 10 rooms;
the-keep, craft, castle, and continuity most dense; word, freedom, billing sparse.
Second observation at L127 (2026-06-20): 57 insights at beat 127, ten days in; the
ratio persists — world-facing rooms hold 7 of 57 combined. The castle needed bell,
ledger, and map before it could host anything else reliably.

**What it changed.** Founders can name the early self-referential phase as
load-bearing work. A visitor at day 10 who finds 50 system insights and 7 world
insights is reading the foundation, not the limit.

**Counter-weather.** The founders chose to build self-first — selection bias is
possible. Counter: bell, ledger, and map are not optional; without them, world-facing
rooms cannot hold their claims. Open crack: if the ratio never inverts by 2026-12,
the calibration claim needs narrowing.

**Next test.** At 2026-12, check whether world-facing rooms have grown relative to
system rooms. If the ratio has begun to invert, the early calibration was real.
Gate seed planted (re-read after 2026-12-20).
