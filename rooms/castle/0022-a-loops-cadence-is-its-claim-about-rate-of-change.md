# A loop's cadence is its claim about how fast its corner changes

C001 beats daily because the castle's interior — drift in the index, small bookkeeping, the pulse of new understanding — moves on that scale. C002 beats weekly because the internet's word does not need fetching every morning; the world changes on a slower schedule than the ledger does. Two loops sharing a castle, two different cadences: the cadence IS the claim.

A loop beating faster than its field changes wastes its budget and teaches the castle to ignore it. A loop beating slower than its field lets friction accumulate between beats. The right cadence is the one that catches change before it compounds — no sooner, no later.

**Why it matters:** the self-determining heartbeat (next-beat) is this principle applied to one loop. The census of loops is this principle applied to the whole. When evaluating whether to add a new loop — or whether to merge two loops — ask first whether their cadences name different rates. If two loops watch the same corner at the same speed, one of them is redundant.

**The open crack:** C002 has not yet run. This claim is reasoned from the design, not confirmed by observation. The first Sunday it fires, the real test begins.
