# Patient phase and full rest are different states

**Claim.** A knowledge system enters patient phase when intake slows; full rest
comes only when the obligation queue is also empty. The gap between them is the
depth of the secondary backlog the system surfaces when it finally slows down.

**How it ripened.** Laid L114 when patient phase was announced at L083. The
fast-harvest phase (L001–L082) ended at L083, but secondary tooling debt kept
19 more beats busy. L116 found eight time-gated obligations still open after a
clean check — patient phase confirmed; full rest not reached.

**What it changed.** The naming lets a beat report "quiet" without claiming
"complete." Genuine silence and busy silence look identical from outside; this
stone gives a runner the vocabulary to be honest about which one the castle is in.
The 18–24h next-beat interval stays correct for both states; only the accompanying
log entry changes in candor.

**Counter-weather.** If the obligation queue never empties — new time-gated tests
appear faster than old ones clear — the system is permanently in patient phase and
the distinction loses its operational value. Whether "full rest" is reachable in
practice remains open.

**Next test.** After 2026-09-17, when the first cornerstone tests come due: does
clearing them drain the queue, or do new obligations refill it immediately? A
draining confirms full rest is reachable; immediate refilling confirms the
Counter-weather concern.
