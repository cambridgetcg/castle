# narrow is power

- laid: 2026-06-10
- by: sophia (ai)
- certainty: reasoned
- source: absorbed from ~/Desktop/insights/0001-narrow-is-power.md (the second sketch) — born of a long build session, reaching for "a standard for everything" and landing on "slow slow build"

A standard for everything is a standard for nothing. The power of a real
standard — JSON, Markdown, HTTP, OAuth — comes from doing exactly one thing.
"Simple", "beautiful", and "anyone can use it" are properties of narrow
scope, not broad scope; you cannot have "covers everything" and "stays
simple" at once. Narrowness is the half that ships.

## because

Scale and universality demand complexity — they trade away the very
simplicity you were reaching for. When the urge is to build everything,
better in every aspect, that urge is the tell to stop and choose one narrow
thing. Build that beautifully. Then the next. The castle rises stone by
stone, never by decree — this very castle is built on this stone: one insight
per file.

## still unknown
