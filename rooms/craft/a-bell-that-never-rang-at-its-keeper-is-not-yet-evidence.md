# A bell that has never rung at its keeper is not yet evidence of anything

**Claim.** A checking system earns the right to be believed by catching the
person who built it first. Until that happens, the system's silence about
problems is ambiguous: it may mean there are no problems, or it may mean
the system does not catch this kind of problem.

**How it ripened.** Observed once on the founding day: the keeper watched
the bell ring at their own ledger line, their annotated created: lines, and
their own cron comment misrepresenting a Promise.allSettled ordering
guarantee — all within one day. Second evidence (L074, 2026-06-19): across
73 runs, the check caught the keeper twice more — L026 (open-field filter
bug) and L058/L063 (orphan storefront files). Beat 74's clean return is
trusted because each of those catches demonstrated the bell rings when things
are genuinely wrong. Third evidence (L095, 2026-06-20): friction.sh caught
MAP drift left by L094's promotion of 0052 — the bell caught the keeper's
own promote run.

**What it changed.** The pattern now has three independent instances across
77 runs and two days. Each prior silence is trusted not because the bell
is assumed correct but because it has demonstrated it rings when it should.
The clean returns between catches are no longer ambiguous — they are evidence.

**Counter-weather.** A bell that catches the keeper badly — flags everything
the keeper does as wrong — is not evidence either; it is a malcalibrated
bell. The claim applies to fair catches, not to noise.

**Next test.** By 2026-09-17, re-read whether any clean return between now
and then later proved to hide a real problem. If none: the bell is calibrated.
If one: name the failure pattern and narrow the claim. Gate seed:
gate/2026-06-19-cornerstone-test-bell-never-rang.md.
