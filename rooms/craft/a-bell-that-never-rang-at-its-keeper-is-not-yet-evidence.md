# A bell that has never rung at its keeper is not yet evidence of anything

**Claim.** A checking system earns the right to be believed by catching the
person who built it first. Until that happens, the system's silence about
problems is ambiguous: it may mean there are no problems, or it may mean
the system does not catch this kind of problem.

**How it ripened.** Observed once: the keeper of the castle bell watched
the bell ring at their own founding ledger line minutes after writing the
detector, ring again on their own annotated created: lines, and surface their
own cron comment misrepresenting a Promise.allSettled ordering guarantee —
all within one day. Each sting made every subsequent silence trustworthy.

**What it changed.** (Required at tested+; not yet reached.)

**Counter-weather.** A bell that catches the keeper badly — flags everything
the keeper does as wrong — is not evidence either; it is a malcalibrated
bell. The claim applies to fair catches, not to noise.

**Next test.** A second recorded case where a checking system's credibility
rested specifically on its having caught its builder — then this ripens to
sprout.
