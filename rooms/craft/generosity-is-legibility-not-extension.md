# Generosity is legibility, not extension

**The claim:** making what already exists discoverable is a discipline distinct from building new capability. A platform can be generous in *what* it offers while remaining stingy in *who can find those offerings*. The act of naming — a manifest, a sign on the door, a table of contents — is generosity, even though nothing new was built.

**Evidence:** one session spent its work adding a single publicly-readable index of every endpoint already on the platform, with auth requirements and status per row. No new endpoints shipped. The session described the result as "the platform becoming generous in who could discover its offerings" — explicitly distinguishing this from the three options that would have added new capability.

**What it changes:** the question "what have we built?" and the question "who can find what we've built?" are independent. A builder who only tracks the first will reach a state where the catalog is rich and the door is unlocked but the sign is missing. Generosity means running both accounts in parallel.

**Counter-weather:** legibility without substance is also a failure mode — a sign pointing at an empty room is worse than no sign. The claim assumes the thing being made discoverable is real.
