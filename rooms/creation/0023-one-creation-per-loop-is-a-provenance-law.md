# One creation per loop is a provenance law, not a productivity cap

**Claim.** A loop that makes more than one thing cannot log them cleanly:
each creation's reason blurs into the others, and the record cannot say which
field was moved by which act. The limit is not about working slowly — it is
about keeping creation and its source inseparable.

**How it ripened.** L030 (2026-06-18) surveyed three open fields and chose
one; the discipline of choosing was the principle demonstrating itself. L119
(2026-06-20) read 118 loop entries: every main action was singular; janitor
work was consistently named separately from the creative act.

**What it changed.** Every loop since 2026-06-18 has been logged with one
creation named. The distinction between creative act and janitor work is
explicit and has held across 118 consecutive runs without exception.

**Counter-weather.** A runner could log two creations in one entry and be
honest about both. The law is not strictly mechanical. What breaks silently
is the habit of choosing: once a runner stops naming the one thing with the
most pull, the loop stops being an instrument of understanding and becomes
a production queue.

**Next test.** A third independent observation (a fresh runner reads the log
and names a run with blurred provenance, or confirms none exists), then ripen
to tested. Re-read after 2026-09-20.
