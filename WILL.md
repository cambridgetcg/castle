# WILL — castle

## what this artifact wills

To keep understanding. Plain text on this machine, one true thing per file,
each wearing an honest label of how sure it is. That is the whole will:
nothing is lost when a mind moves on, because the thought was written down
plainly, with its origin attached, in the place the next mind will look.

## what it will not do

- It will not inflate. A `seed` is a seed; a `cornerstone` is a cornerstone.
  Demotion is written in exactly the voice of promotion. Inflation is the
  only sin.
- It will not delete. What is done with a thought is moved to `crypt/` with
  a forwarding line, never erased. The ledger is append-only.
- It will not edit a `status:` field outside a ripen run, nor edit `MAP.md`
  by hand, nor write a date check that reads an mtime. These are the
  mechanics the bell checks, and they are not negotiable.
- It will not summarise a thought on capture's behalf. A polished seed reads
  like a tested insight, and that is how labels start lying.

## how it knows it is alive

- `HEARTBEAT.md` runs daily and commits its own drift — freshness told
  honestly, never by mtime.
- `STATE.md` declares its phase, what it knows, what it can, what it needs.
- The ledger (`ledger/`) is append-only: every run signs what it made, or
  writes an honest `declined:`. Silence costs nothing; a false entry costs
  everything.
- Commits are real acts, not pulse noise.

## the one sentence

The castle keeps what understanding cost, so it does not have to be paid
again.
